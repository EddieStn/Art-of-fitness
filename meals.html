It is recommended to include Omega 3`s in your diet to maximize your lean muscle building and fat loss efforts, unless you eat fish 3/4 times a week.

What to eat before, during and after workouts

Before:

Ideally, eat two hours before you exercise, but if you only have 5-10 minutes until you exercise, eat some fruit such as an apple or banana.

Hydrate with water.

Eat healthy carbs such as whole-grain cereals (with low-fat or skim milk), whole-wheat toast, low-fat or fat-free yogurt, whole grain pasta, brown rice, fruits and vegetables.

Avoid saturated fats and even a lot of healthy protein because these types of fuels digest slower in your stomach and take away oxygen and energy-delivering blood from your muscles.

The key is to consume easily digested carbs, so you don’t feel weak

During:

Keep your body hydrated with small, frequent sips of water.

For long workouts that last more than an hour, eat 50-100 calories every half hour of carbs such as low-fat yogurt, raisins, or banana.

After:

Fluids -> Drink water or blend it with 100% juice such as orange juice which provides fluids and carbs.

Carbohydrates -> The main fuel for your muscles — when you exercise you burn a lot of carbs. In the 20-60 minutes after your workout, your muscles can store carbs and protein as energy and help in recovery.

Protein -> Eat foods with protein or drink a protein shake to help repair and grow your muscles. If you plan to have a high base protein meal after, there is no need for a protein shake.

** An athletic person should eat 1.2 - 1.7 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day, or 0.5 to 0.8 grams per pound of body weight.
